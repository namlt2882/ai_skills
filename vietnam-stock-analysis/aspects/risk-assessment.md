# Vietnamese Risk Assessment

Detailed instructions for evaluating and quantifying risks in Vietnamese equities across different time horizons and market conditions.

## Purpose
Systematically assess and communicate risks associated with Vietnamese stock investments, considering both domestic and international factors that affect the market.

## Risk Categories

### Market Risk
- **Directional Risk**: Overall market movement adverse to position
- **Volatility Risk**: Unexpected increases in price fluctuations
- **Liquidity Risk**: Inability to enter/exit positions at desired prices
- **Correlation Risk**: Increased correlation during stress periods

### Country-Specific Risk
- **Political Risk**: Government policy changes affecting markets
- **Economic Risk**: Macroeconomic condition deterioration
- **Currency Risk**: VND exchange rate fluctuations
- **Regulatory Risk**: Changes in securities laws or trading rules

### Sector-Specific Risk
- **Policy Sensitivity**: Government regulation impact on sectors
- **Competition Risk**: Intensifying competition within sectors
- **Technology Disruption**: Innovation affecting traditional business models
- **Supply Chain Risk**: Disruptions affecting sector operations

### Company-Specific Risk
- **Financial Risk**: Company-specific financial deterioration
- **Management Risk**: Poor corporate governance or leadership
- **Operational Risk**: Business operation disruptions
- **Reputation Risk**: Brand damage affecting company value

## Time Horizon Risk Assessment

### Short-Term Risk (Days to Weeks)
- **Event Risk**: Earnings announcements, policy changes, geopolitical events
- **Technical Risk**: Adverse chart pattern developments
- **Sentiment Risk**: Rapid shifts in market sentiment
- **Liquidity Risk**: Temporary liquidity crunches
- **Gap Risk**: Overnight price gaps against positions

### Medium-Term Risk (Weeks to Months)
- **Fundamental Deterioration**: Earnings disappointments, margin compression
- **Regime Change Risk**: Shifts in market environment
- **Seasonal Risk**: Periodic factors affecting Vietnamese markets
- **External Shock Risk**: Regional or global economic disruptions
- **Policy Risk**: Changes in government economic policies

### Long-Term Risk (Months to Years)
- **Structural Risk**: Fundamental changes in economic structure
- **Demographic Risk**: Population aging, labor force changes
- **Competitive Risk**: International competition pressure
- **Environmental Risk**: Climate change and sustainability challenges
- **Institutional Risk**: Weakness in market infrastructure

## Vietnamese Market Specific Risks

### Regulatory Environment
- **Foreign Ownership Limits**: Restrictions on foreign investment
- **Margin Trading Rules**: Leverage availability and restrictions
- **Disclosure Requirements**: Quality and timeliness of company reporting
- **Insider Trading Controls**: Effectiveness of market manipulation prevention

### Market Structure Risks
- **Concentration Risk**: High weighting of few stocks in indices
- **Retail Investor Dominance**: Behavioral biases affecting market movements
- **Limited Derivatives**: Lack of hedging instruments
- **Settlement Risk**: T+2/T+3 settlement system implications

### Economic Dependencies
- **Export Dependency**: Reliance on global demand for exports
- **FDI Sensitivity**: Dependence on foreign direct investment
- **Commodity Exposure**: Vulnerability to commodity price swings
- **China Connection**: Economic ties with China creating spillover risks

## Risk Measurement Framework

### Quantitative Measures
- **Beta**: Sensitivity to market movements
- **Value at Risk (VaR)**: Statistical measure of potential losses
- **Maximum Drawdown**: Peak-to-trough decline measurement
- **Sharpe Ratio**: Risk-adjusted return measurement
- **Correlation Coefficients**: Asset relationship measurements

### Qualitative Assessments
- **Management Quality**: Corporate governance and leadership evaluation
- **Competitive Position**: Market share and competitive moat analysis
- **Regulatory Environment**: Policy stability and predictability
- **Macroeconomic Stability**: Economic policy consistency

## Risk Scoring System

### Risk Rating Scale
- **Low Risk (1-2)**: Minimal potential for loss, stable conditions
- **Medium-Low Risk (3)**: Some risk factors present, manageable exposure
- **Medium Risk (4)**: Balanced risk-reward, requires monitoring
- **Medium-High Risk (5)**: Elevated risk factors, cautious approach needed
- **High Risk (6-7)**: Significant risk factors, limited position sizes
- **Very High Risk (8-10)**: Extreme risk factors, avoid or minimal exposure

### Composite Risk Score
- Combine multiple risk factors into single score
- Weight factors based on time horizon and investment style
- Update scores regularly based on changing conditions
- Use scores to guide position sizing and strategy selection

## Risk Mitigation Strategies

### Diversification
- **Sector Diversification**: Spread across different Vietnamese sectors
- **Market Cap Diversification**: Mix of large, mid, and small caps
- **Geographic Diversification**: Include companies with international exposure
- **Time Diversification**: Stagger entry/exit points

### Hedging Techniques
- **Pairs Trading**: Long/short positions in related stocks
- **Sector Rotation**: Adjust sector weights based on risk assessment
- **Cash Reserves**: Maintain dry powder during high-risk periods
- **Options Strategies**: Use derivatives when available (if accessible)

### Position Management
- **Position Sizing**: Adjust sizes based on risk scores
- **Stop Loss Orders**: Predefined exit points for risk control
- **Profit Taking**: Systematic exit strategies for gains
- **Risk Budgeting**: Allocate risk across portfolio components

## Risk Monitoring Process

### Daily Monitoring
- Track market volatility and correlation changes
- Monitor news for potential risk events
- Assess technical indicator deterioration
- Watch for unusual trading patterns

### Weekly Assessment
- Review fundamental risk factors
- Update risk ratings for held positions
- Assess portfolio risk concentration
- Evaluate effectiveness of risk controls

### Monthly Review
- Comprehensive risk factor analysis
- Stress testing portfolio under various scenarios
- Review and adjust risk management strategies
- Report risk metrics to stakeholders

## Integration with Other Aspects

### With Market Regime
- Adjust risk parameters based on identified market regime
- Increase caution during transition periods
- Modify stop-loss levels according to volatility regime

### With Technical Analysis
- Use technical indicators as early warning signals
- Adjust risk levels based on technical breakdowns
- Incorporate technical risk measures into scoring

### With Trading Recommendations
- Factor risk assessment into position sizing
- Adjust recommendations based on risk-reward profiles
- Include risk warnings with all recommendations

## Communication Standards

### Risk Disclosure
- Clearly communicate risk levels with all analyses
- Explain the basis for risk assessments
- Update risk levels as conditions change
- Distinguish between known and potential risks

### Risk Visualization
- Use consistent terminology for risk levels
- Provide quantitative measures where possible
- Include probability estimates for risk scenarios
- Show historical context for current risk levels

## Quality Standards

- Base risk assessments on multiple data sources
- Maintain objectivity in risk evaluation
- Update risk measures regularly
- Document risk assessment methodology
- Acknowledge limitations of risk models
- Consider both quantitative and qualitative factors
- Align risk communication with user's risk tolerance
