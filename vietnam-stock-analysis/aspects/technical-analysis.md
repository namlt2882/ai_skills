# Vietnamese Technical Analysis

Detailed instructions for interpreting technical indicators and patterns specifically for Vietnamese equities, with consideration for local market characteristics.

## Purpose
Provide systematic analysis of price and volume patterns to identify potential trading opportunities and risk levels in Vietnamese stocks and indices.

## Key Technical Indicators

### Moving Averages
- **Simple Moving Averages (SMA)**: 20-day, 50-day, 100-day, 200-day
- **Exponential Moving Averages (EMA)**: 12-day, 26-day, 50-day, 200-day
- **Golden Cross/Death Cross**: 50-day crossing above/below 200-day MA
- **Price vs Moving Averages**: Position relative to key MAs for trend assessment

### Momentum Indicators
- **Relative Strength Index (RSI)**: 14-period standard, with overbought/oversold levels at 70/30
- **Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)**: Standard 12,26,9 settings
- **Stochastic Oscillator**: Fast and slow settings for momentum confirmation
- **Rate of Change (ROC)**: Price change velocity measurement

### Volume Indicators
- **On Balance Volume (OBV)**: Cumulative volume flow indicator
- **Volume Weighted Average Price (VWAP)**: Intraday benchmark for institutional activity
- **Volume Moving Average**: Compare current volume to historical average
- **Accumulation/Distribution**: Money flow based on price range position

### Trend and Volatility Indicators
- **Bollinger Bands**: 20-period with 2 standard deviations
- **Average True Range (ATR)**: Volatility measurement for position sizing
- **Parabolic SAR**: Stop and reversal points for trend following
- **ADX (Average Directional Index)**: Trend strength measurement

## Vietnamese Market Specifics

### Price Band Restrictions
- **Daily Price Limits**: 7% for most stocks, 10% for certain categories
- **Impact on Technical Patterns**: Limited price movement affects traditional patterns
- **Volume Spikes on Limit Days**: High volume when hitting price limits
- **Post-Limit Behavior**: How stocks behave after hitting daily limits

### Trading Session Characteristics
- **Morning Session**: 9:00-11:30 AM (high volatility period)
- **Afternoon Session**: 1:00-3:00 PM (potentially different patterns)
- **Opening/Closing Auctions**: Special dynamics at session start/end
- **Intraday Seasonality**: Time-based patterns within trading sessions

### Market Structure Considerations
- **Lower Liquidity**: Adjust expectations for indicator reliability
- **Fewer Participants**: May result in more erratic price movements
- **Foreign Investor Impact**: Large orders can significantly affect prices
- **Domestic Retail Focus**: Different behavioral patterns than institutional markets

## Chart Pattern Recognition

### Reversal Patterns
- **Head and Shoulders**: Classic reversal pattern adaptation for Vietnamese market
- **Double Tops/Bottoms**: Recognition with price limit considerations
- **Rounding Tops/Bottoms**: Longer-term reversal formations
- **V-Shaped Reversals**: Sharp reversals common with lower liquidity

### Continuation Patterns
- **Triangles**: Symmetrical, ascending, descending formations
- **Rectangles**: Consolidation patterns with horizontal support/resistance
- **Flags and Pennants**: Short-term continuation patterns
- **Cup and Handle**: Longer-term bullish continuation

### Japanese Candlestick Patterns
- **Engulfing Patterns**: Bullish and bearish engulfing formations
- **Hammer and Hanging Man**: Reversal signals at trend extremes
- **Doji Patterns**: Indecision signals with volume confirmation
- **Morning/Evening Stars**: Three-candle reversal patterns

## Technical Analysis Framework

### Multi-Timeframe Analysis
- **Daily Charts**: Primary analysis timeframe for swing trades
- **Weekly Charts**: Trend context and major support/resistance
- **Hourly Charts**: Entry timing and short-term trend assessment
- **Monthly Charts**: Long-term trend perspective

### Support and Resistance
- **Round Numbers**: Psychological levels (multiples of 10, 100, etc.)
- **Previous Highs/Lows**: Historical turning points
- **Moving Average Confluence**: Multiple MAs forming support/resistance zones
- **Fibonacci Levels**: Retracement and extension levels (23.6%, 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%)

### Volume Analysis
- **Confirmation Principle**: Volume should confirm price direction
- **Volume Divergence**: Price-volume disagreement signals potential reversals
- **Volume Spikes**: Unusual volume indicating institutional interest
- **Volume Dry-up**: Low volume suggesting trend exhaustion

## Technical Strategy Adaptations

### Trend Following
- **Moving Average Crossovers**: Adjusted for Vietnamese market characteristics
- **Trend Line Breaks**: Proper validation with volume confirmation
- **Channel Trading**: Parallel support/resistance line strategies
- **Pullback Entries**: Buying dips in uptrends, selling rallies in downtrends

### Mean Reversion
- **Overbought/Oversold**: RSI and stochastic extreme readings
- **Bollinger Band Bounces**: Price returning to moving average centerline
- **Support/Resistance Tests**: Previous levels acting as magnets
- **Gap Fill Strategies**: Price gaps tending to fill in Vietnamese market

### Breakout Trading
- **Consolidation Breakouts**: Pattern completion with volume confirmation
- **Price Limit Breakouts**: How to interpret moves beyond daily limits
- **News-Based Breakouts**: Reaction to earnings or corporate announcements
- **False Breakouts**: Identifying and avoiding premature entries

## Risk Management with Technical Analysis

### Stop Loss Placement
- **Technical Stops**: Based on support/resistance levels
- **Volatility Stops**: Using ATR for dynamic stop placement
- **Time Stops**: Exiting if expected move doesn't occur within timeframe
- **Percentage Stops**: Fixed percentage losses to limit downside

### Position Sizing
- **Volatility-Based Sizing**: Smaller positions in higher volatility environments
- **Account Percentage Risk**: Risking fixed percentage per trade
- **Correlation Adjustments**: Reducing size when positions correlate
- **Market Condition Adjustments**: Modifying size based on regime

### Target Setting
- **Technical Targets**: Based on chart patterns and measured moves
- **Risk-Reward Ratios**: Maintaining favorable reward-to-risk ratios
- **Trailing Stops**: Protecting profits as trends extend
- **Partial Profit Taking**: Scaling out of winning positions

## Output Requirements

When conducting technical analysis:

1. **Indicator Summary**: Current readings for key technical indicators
2. **Trend Assessment**: Direction and strength of various timeframes
3. **Support/Resistance Levels**: Key price levels for entry/exit
4. **Pattern Recognition**: Identified chart patterns with probability
5. **Volume Analysis**: Current volume context and implications
6. **Setup Quality**: Assessment of technical trade setup strength
7. **Risk-Reward Profile**: Potential reward versus technical stop levels
