# Key Concepts and Data Sources for US Financial Market Analysis

## Core Concepts

### Intermarket Analysis Principles
- **John Murphy's Four Pillars**: Stocks, Bonds, Commodities, Currencies
- **Leading vs Lagging Markets**: Identifying which markets lead trend changes
- **Correlation Analysis**: Dynamic relationships between asset classes
- **Business Cycle Applications**: How intermarket relationships change with economic cycles

### Risk-On/Risk-Off Regimes
- **Risk Asset Classification**: Equities, corporate bonds, commodities, EM assets
- **Safe Haven Assets**: Government bonds, USD, JPY, CHF, gold
- **Flight to Quality**: Capital movement patterns during stress
- **Volatility Regimes**: VIX and other fear gauges

### Technical Analysis Foundations
- **Trend Following**: Moving averages, trendlines, channels
- **Momentum Indicators**: RSI, MACD, stochastic oscillator
- **Volume Analysis**: OBV, volume patterns, accumulation/distribution
- **Volatility Measures**: Bollinger Bands, ATR, implied volatility

### Macroeconomic Drivers
- **Monetary Policy**: Federal Reserve actions and communications
- **Fiscal Policy**: Government spending and taxation impacts
- **Economic Indicators**: GDP, employment, inflation, PMI
- **Global Factors**: International trade, currency movements, geopolitical events

## Essential Data Sources

### Government and Central Bank Sources
- **Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED)**: https://fred.stlouisfed.org/
  - Interest rates, monetary aggregates, economic indicators
  - Yield curve data, employment statistics, inflation measures
  - International economic data and exchange rates

- **Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS)**: https://www.bls.gov/
  - Employment situation (NFP, unemployment rate)
  - Consumer Price Index (CPI) and Producer Price Index (PPI)
  - Productivity and compensation data

- **Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA)**: https://www.bea.gov/
  - Gross Domestic Product (GDP) data and components
  - Personal income and consumption expenditures (PCE)
  - International trade and investment data

- **Federal Reserve**: https://www.federalreserve.gov/
  - FOMC statements and meeting minutes
  - Federal funds rate decisions
  - Beige Book economic conditions report
  - Balance sheet and monetary policy communications

### Market Data Sources
- **CBOE**: https://www.cboe.com/
  - VIX and volatility indices
  - Options data and implied volatility
  - Market sentiment indicators

- **CFTC Commitment of Traders**: https://www.cftc.gov/MarketReports/CommitmentsofTraders/index.htm
  - Position data for futures markets
  - Commercial vs speculative positioning
  - Extreme positioning as contrarian signals

- **Treasury International Capital (TIC)**: https://www.treasury.gov/resource-center/data-chart-center/tic/Pages/index.aspx
  - Foreign investment in US securities
  - Capital flow patterns and trends

### International Sources
- **OECD**: https://www.oecd.org/
  - Leading economic indicators
  - Composite indicators for major economies
  - Economic outlook and analysis

- **IMF**: https://www.imf.org/
  - Global economic projections
  - Financial stability reports
  - Currency and trade analysis

- **BIS**: https://www.bis.org/
  - International banking statistics
  - Currency and derivatives market data
  - Financial stability analysis

## Key Market Indicators

### Equity Market Indicators
- **Major Indices**: S&P 500, Dow Jones, NASDAQ, Russell 2000
- **Sector ETFs**: XLF (financials), XLK (technology), XLE (energy), XLB (materials)
- **Market Breadth**: Advance/decline line, new highs/lows
- **Sentiment**: AAII investor sentiment, put/call ratios

### Fixed Income Indicators
- **Treasury Yields**: 2-year, 5-year, 10-year, 30-year
- **Yield Curve Slope**: 10-year minus 2-year spread
- **Credit Spreads**: CDX IG, CDX HY, MOVE index
- **TIPS Breakevens**: Inflation expectations measures

### Currency Indicators
- **DXY**: US Dollar Index (vs major currencies)
- **Major Crosses**: EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY, USD/CHF
- **Commodity Dollars**: AUD/USD, NZD/USD, USD/CAD
- **Emerging Market Currencies**: USD/MXN, USD/ZAR, USD/BRL

### Commodity Indicators
- **Energy**: Crude oil (WTI, Brent), gasoline, heating oil
- **Metals**: Gold, silver, copper, platinum
- **Agriculture**: Corn, wheat, soybeans, cotton
- **Industrial**: Aluminum, zinc, nickel

## Economic Calendar Events

### High-Impact Events
- **FOMC Meetings**: Interest rate decisions (8 times/year)
- **NFP Employment Report**: First Friday of each month
- **CPI and PCE**: Mid-month inflation data
- **GDP Releases**: Quarterly with revisions
- **Treasury Auctions**: Regular schedule affecting yields

### Medium-Impact Events
- **ISM PMI Reports**: Monthly manufacturing and services
- **Housing Data**: Starts, permits, existing/new home sales
- **Retail Sales**: Monthly consumer spending
- **Consumer Confidence**: Conference Board and Michigan sentiment

### Continuous Monitoring
- **Weekly Jobless Claims**: Weekly labor market data
- **EIA Petroleum Status Report**: Weekly oil inventory data
- **Fed Speaker Events**: Speeches and testimonies
- **Corporate Earnings**: Quarterly reporting seasons

## Analytical Tools and Frameworks

### Intermarket Analysis Framework
1. **Asset Class Correlation Matrix**: Dynamic correlations between stocks, bonds, commodities, currencies
2. **Relative Strength Analysis**: Comparing performance across asset classes
3. **Leading Indicator Identification**: Which markets lead others in trend changes
4. **Regime Detection**: Identifying shifts in intermarket relationships

### Risk Assessment Framework
1. **Volatility Regime Classification**: Low, normal, high volatility environments
2. **Correlation Stress Testing**: How relationships change during market stress
3. **Liquidity Assessment**: Market depth and trading conditions
4. **Tail Risk Evaluation**: Extreme event probability and impact

### Technical Analysis Toolkit
1. **Trend Analysis**: Multiple timeframe moving averages and trendlines
2. **Momentum Analysis**: Oscillators and divergence detection
3. **Volume Analysis**: Money flow and accumulation/distribution
4. **Pattern Recognition**: Chart patterns and behavioral formations

### Macroeconomic Assessment
1. **Policy Cycle Analysis**: Monetary and fiscal policy stance
2. **Economic Cycle Positioning**: Where in business cycle
3. **Inflation Dynamics**: Drivers and implications
4. **Global Coordination**: International economic synchronization

## Risk Management Considerations

### Position Sizing
- **Volatility-Based Sizing**: Adjusting position size based on ATR or volatility measures
- **Correlation Awareness**: Understanding how positions move together
- **Regime-Aware Sizing**: Adjusting based on market environment
- **Portfolio-Level Risk**: Managing total portfolio exposure

### Diversification Principles
- **Uncorrelated Assets**: Including assets with low correlation
- **Regime Diversification**: Positions that work in different market environments
- **Time Diversification**: Different holding periods and rebalancing frequencies
- **Strategy Diversification**: Multiple approaches to market participation

### Hedging Strategies
- **Beta Hedging**: Using index futures for market exposure
- **Currency Hedging**: Managing foreign exchange exposure
- **Volatility Hedging**: Using VIX products or options
- **Correlation Hedging**: Positions that benefit from relationship changes

## Performance Measurement

### Absolute Performance Metrics
- **Total Return**: Price appreciation plus income
- **Risk-Adjusted Returns**: Sharpe ratio, Sortino ratio
- **Maximum Drawdown**: Peak-to-trough decline
- **Volatility**: Standard deviation of returns

### Relative Performance Metrics
- **Benchmark Comparison**: vs relevant indices
- **Peer Group Analysis**: vs similar strategies
- **Market Environment Performance**: performance in different regimes
- **Correlation Analysis**: how returns relate to various factors

### Attribution Analysis
- **Asset Allocation**: returns from strategic decisions
- **Security Selection**: returns from tactical decisions
- **Market Timing**: returns from exposure changes
- **Interaction Effects**: combined impact of decisions
