# Risk Assets Analysis Framework

## Overview

Risk assets analysis focuses on understanding the behavior of investments that carry higher risk and volatility compared to safer alternatives. This analysis examines how different asset classes behave during various market conditions, particularly during risk-on and risk-off periods.

## Risk-On vs Risk-Off Regimes

### Risk-On Environment
Characteristics:
- Investors seek higher returns despite higher risk
- Equities generally advance
- Credit spreads tighten
- Commodity prices rise
- Emerging market currencies strengthen
- USD weakens (typically)
- Yields rise (growth expectations)
- VIX declines

### Risk-Off Environment
Characteristics:
- Investors flee to safety and preservation
- Bonds rally (especially government bonds)
- Equities decline
- Commodity prices fall
- USD strengthens (traditional safe haven)
- Emerging market currencies weaken
- Yields decline (flight to quality)
- VIX rises

## Asset Classification

### Risk Assets
- **Equities**: Domestic and international stocks
- **Corporate Bonds**: High yield and investment grade corporates
- **Emerging Market Assets**: EM equities, bonds, and currencies
- **Commodities**: Oil, industrial metals, agricultural products
- **Real Estate**: REITs and property investments
- **Cryptocurrencies**: Bitcoin, Ethereum, and altcoins

### Safe Haven Assets
- **Government Bonds**: US Treasuries, German Bunds, Japanese Government Bonds
- **Currencies**: USD, JPY, CHF (traditional safe havens)
- **Precious Metals**: Gold, silver (inflation and currency debasement hedges)
- **Cash**: Physical cash and money market instruments
- **Defensive Stocks**: Utilities, consumer staples, healthcare

## Flight to Quality Phenomena

### Definition
Flight to quality occurs when investors sell riskier assets and purchase safer ones during times of market stress or uncertainty.

### Common Patterns
1. **Bond Market**: Investors move from corporate bonds to government bonds
2. **Currency Market**: Capital flows from emerging market currencies to developed market safe haven currencies
3. **Equity Market**: Rotation from cyclical to defensive sectors
4. **Alternative Assets**: Movement from commodities to precious metals

### Triggers for Flight to Quality
- Geopolitical tensions
- Economic recession fears
- Financial system instability
- Pandemic or health crises
- Natural disasters
- Political uncertainty

## Correlation Analysis

### Positive Correlations (Normal Conditions)
- Stocks and commodities often move together
- Bonds and defensive stocks may move together
- Risk assets tend to be positively correlated during stable periods

### Correlation Breakdowns (Stress Conditions)
- Traditional correlations can break down during crises
- Assets that normally have low correlation may become highly correlated
- Safe haven assets may lose their protective qualities temporarily

### Dynamic Correlations
- Correlations change based on market regime
- Economic cycle affects asset relationships
- Policy changes can alter traditional correlations

## Key Risk Indicators

### Volatility Measures
- **VIX (Volatility Index)**: Fear gauge of the market
- **MOVE Index**: Bond market volatility
- **Currency Volatility Indices**: FX market stress indicators
- **Sector Volatility**: Individual sector risk measures

### Sentiment Indicators
- **Put/Call Ratios**: Options market sentiment
- **AAII Sentiment Survey**: Individual investor sentiment
- **NAAIM Exposure Index**: Professional money manager sentiment
- **CNN Fear & Greed Index**: Composite sentiment measure

### Liquidity Indicators
- **Bid-Ask Spreads**: Widen during stress periods
- **Trading Volumes**: Abnormal volumes indicate stress
- **Price Impact**: How much price moves per unit of volume
- **Market Depth**: Ability to absorb large trades without significant price movement

## Risk Management Techniques

### Diversification Across Regimes
- Maintain positions that perform well in both risk-on and risk-off environments
- Avoid overconcentration in single asset classes
- Consider cross-regime correlation analysis

### Dynamic Allocation
- Adjust portfolio weightings based on regime assessment
- Increase safe haven allocation during risk-off periods
- Reduce risk asset exposure when indicators deteriorate

### Hedging Strategies
- Use derivatives for protection
- Implement volatility targeting
- Consider tail risk protection
- Use inverse ETFs during extreme risk-off periods

## Leading Indicators for Regime Changes

### Economic Leading Indicators
- Yield curve inversions
- Credit spread widening
- Manufacturing PMI trends
- Employment trend changes

### Market-Based Leading Indicators
- High yield bond spreads
- Emerging market debt spreads
- Currency strength patterns
- Commodity demand indicators

### Policy-Based Leading Indicators
- Central bank policy changes
- Fiscal policy shifts
- Regulatory environment changes
- Tax policy announcements

## Safe Haven Effectiveness

### Historical Performance During Crises
- **Gold**: Effective during inflationary periods and currency crises
- **US Treasuries**: Effective during deflationary periods and liquidity crises
- **Japanese Yen**: Effective during global risk-off episodes
- **Swiss Franc**: Effective during European crises
- **USD**: Effective during global crises (exception: USD funding stress)

### Factors Affecting Safe Haven Status
- Liquidity availability
- Market capacity to absorb flows
- Fundamental backdrop (inflation vs deflation)
- Central bank interventions

## Risk-On/Risk-Off Trading Strategies

### Momentum-Based Approaches
- Trend following across asset classes
- Relative strength rotation
- Cross-asset momentum signals

### Mean Reversion Approaches
- Contrarian positioning after extreme moves
- Value-based asset selection
- Volatility-based entry points

### Volatility Targeting
- Adjust position sizes based on volatility regime
- Increase during low volatility periods
- Decrease during high volatility periods

## Risk Signals and Warnings

### Early Warning Signs
- Widening credit spreads
- USD strengthening unexpectedly
- Bond-equity correlation becoming positive
- VIX spiking without obvious cause
- Unusual currency movements

### Confirmation Signals
- Multiple asset classes confirming the trend
- Fundamental deterioration supporting risk-off
- Policy responses indicating stress
- Media attention to potential risks

### False Signals to Avoid
- Temporary volatility spikes
- Technical breakdowns without fundamental support
- Short-term sentiment extremes that reverse quickly
- Currency moves driven by technical factors rather than risk sentiment
