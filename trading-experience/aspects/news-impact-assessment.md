# News Impact Assessment Aspect

This aspect covers systematic frameworks for evaluating news impact on trading decisions.

## News Classification System

### News Impact Levels
- **High Impact**: Central bank decisions, major economic releases (CPI, NFP), geopolitical events
- **Medium Impact**: Corporate earnings, economic data releases, policy announcements
- **Low Impact**: Minor economic indicators, corporate announcements, market commentary

### News Categories
- **Economic Data**: GDP, inflation, employment, retail sales, manufacturing indices
- **Monetary Policy**: Interest rate decisions, quantitative easing, forward guidance
- **Corporate Events**: Earnings reports, mergers/acquisitions, management changes
- **Geopolitical**: Trade policies, sanctions, elections, conflicts
- **Market Structure**: Regulatory changes, exchange rules, trading halts

## Pre-News Assessment Framework

### Event Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Identify scheduled news event and expected release time
- [ ] Review consensus estimates and market expectations
- [ ] Assess potential scenarios (better than expected, as expected, worse than expected)
- [ ] Determine historical volatility impact of similar events
- [ ] Evaluate current market positioning and sentiment
- [ ] Identify key support/resistance levels that may be tested
- [ ] Consider correlation with other assets and markets

### Position Management Before News
- **Reduce exposure**: Scale down positions before high-impact events
- **Tighten stops**: Move stop losses closer to protect profits
- **Hedge positions**: Use options or correlated instruments for protection
- **Close positions**: Exit entirely if risk/reward is unfavorable
- **Wait and see**: Stay in cash until clarity emerges

## Real-Time News Evaluation

### Immediate Impact Assessment (0-15 minutes)
- **Actual vs. Expected**: Compare actual data to consensus estimates
- **Market reaction**: Observe price action, volume, and volatility spikes
- **Sentiment shift**: Monitor changes in market sentiment and positioning
- **Cross-market impact**: Check reaction in related markets (bonds, currencies, commodities)
- **Liquidity conditions**: Assess if liquidity has dried up or expanded

### Secondary Impact Assessment (15-60 minutes)
- **Trend confirmation**: Determine if initial reaction is sustainable
- **Reversal potential**: Identify potential for mean reversion
- **Follow-through**: Evaluate if initial move has momentum
- **Market breadth**: Check if move is broad-based or isolated
- **Institutional response**: Monitor large player positioning

### Extended Impact Assessment (1-4 hours)
- **Structural changes**: Identify if news creates new market regime
- **Technical levels**: Note which key levels have been breached
- **Trend continuation**: Determine if existing trends are reinforced or reversed
- **Correlation shifts**: Observe changes in asset correlations
- **Volatility regime**: Assess if volatility has permanently increased

## Post-News Trading Strategies

### Trading the Initial Reaction
- **Fade the move**: Trade against initial overreaction if it appears extreme
- **Momentum trade**: Follow the initial move if it shows strong conviction
- **Wait for confirmation**: Let the market establish direction before entering
- **Trade the retest**: Enter on retest of broken levels

### Trading the Secondary Reaction
- **Breakout trade**: Enter on breakout of consolidation formed after news
- **Continuation trade**: Enter on pullback in direction of initial move
- **Reversal trade**: Enter on signs of trend exhaustion
- **Range trade**: Trade within established range if no clear direction

### Position Sizing After News
- **Conservative sizing**: Use smaller positions during high volatility
- **Volatility-adjusted**: Adjust size based on new volatility levels
- **Scenario-based**: Size positions based on probability of scenarios
- **Risk-based**: Calculate size based on new risk parameters

## News Impact Decision Matrix

### Decision Framework
| Scenario | Market Reaction | Recommended Action |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Actual >> Expected | Strong move in expected direction | Consider momentum trade with tight stop |
| Actual >> Expected | Weak or opposite move | Consider fade trade, wait for confirmation |
| Actual ≈ Expected | Minimal reaction | Avoid trading, wait for clarity |
| Actual << Expected | Strong move in expected direction | Consider momentum trade with tight stop |
| Actual << Expected | Weak or opposite move | Consider fade trade, wait for confirmation |
| Conflicting data | Choppy, directionless action | Stay in cash, wait for clarity |

## News Impact Monitoring

### Ongoing Assessment
- **Track revisions**: Monitor revisions to previous data releases
- **Watch for follow-through**: Note if initial impact sustains or reverses
- **Monitor commentary**: Follow central bank and official statements
- **Check positioning**: Monitor COT reports, positioning data
- **Observe sentiment**: Track sentiment indices and surveys

### Impact Duration Classification
- **Temporary**: Impact lasts minutes to hours (minor data releases)
- **Short-term**: Impact lasts days to weeks (medium impact events)
- **Medium-term**: Impact lasts weeks to months (major policy changes)
- **Long-term**: Impact lasts months to years (structural shifts)
