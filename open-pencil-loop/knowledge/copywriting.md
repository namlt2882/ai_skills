# Copywriting Rules

Guidelines for writing clear, effective copy in designs.

## Text Length Limits

| Element | Max Length | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|
| Headlines | 2-6 words | "Build Faster" |
| Subtitles | 15 words | "Create stunning designs in minutes, not hours." |
| Feature titles | 2-4 words | "Real-time Sync" |
| Feature descriptions | 20 words | "Collaborate with your team instantly with live updates." |
| Button labels | 1-3 words | "Get Started", "Learn More" |
| Card text | 2 sentences | "Streamline your workflow. Save hours every week." |
| Stats | Number + 3 words | "10K+ Active Users" |
| Navigation labels | 1-2 words | "Products", "Pricing" |
| Form labels | 1-3 words | "Email Address", "Full Name" |
| Error messages | 10 words | "Please enter a valid email address." |
| Success messages | 8 words | "Your changes have been saved." |
| Tooltip text | 12 words | "Click to copy the link to clipboard." |

## Writing Style

### Be Direct
- **Good**: "Start your free trial"
- **Bad**: "We would like to invite you to begin your complimentary trial period"

### Use Active Voice
- **Good**: "Download the report"
- **Bad**: "The report can be downloaded"

### Cut Filler Words
Remove: very, really, quite, just, actually, basically, definitely, certainly, probably

### Use Power Words
Replace weak adjectives with strong verbs and nouns:

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| very good | exceptional |
| very fast | lightning-quick |
| very big | massive |
| really easy | effortless |
| quite important | critical |

### One Idea Per Sentence
- **Bad**: "Our platform is easy to use and it has many features and teams love it."
- **Good**: "Our platform is easy to use. Teams love its powerful features."

### Front-Load Important Words
Put the most important information first:
- **Bad**: "You can save time by using our automation features."
- **Good**: "Save time with automation."

## Button Copy

### Use Action Verbs
| Avoid | Use |
|-------|-----|
| Submit | Send, Complete, Save |
| OK | Got it, Understood, Confirm |
| Click here | Get started, Download, View details |
| Learn more | See how it works, View features |

### Create Urgency (sparingly)
- "Start free trial"
- "Get instant access"
- "Join 10,000+ designers"

### Reduce Friction
| High friction | Low friction |
|---------------|--------------|
| Buy now | Try free |
| Register | Get started |
| Create account | Continue with email |

## Headline Patterns

### Benefit-Focused
- "Ship products faster"
- "Close more deals"
- "Build better teams"

### Problem-Solution
- "Stop losing customers"
- "End meeting overload"
- "Fix broken workflows"

### How-To
- "How to scale design systems"
- "The better way to manage projects"

### Social Proof
- "The platform 10,000+ teams trust"
- "Loved by designers at Google, Airbnb, and Stripe"

## Microcopy

### Form Labels
| Generic | Better |
|---------|--------|
| Name | Full name |
| Email | Work email |
| Password | Create password |
| Message | Tell us about your project |

### Placeholders
Use placeholders for examples, not instructions:
- **Bad**: "Enter your email address here"
- **Good**: "you@company.com"

### Empty States
| Generic | Better |
|---------|--------|
| No data | No projects yet. Create your first one. |
| Empty | Start by adding a team member. |
| Nothing here | Upload a file to get started. |

### Error Messages
Be specific and helpful:
- **Bad**: "Error"
- **Better**: "Please enter a valid email"
- **Best**: "We need a valid email to send your receipt"

### Loading States
| Avoid | Use |
|-------|-----|
| Loading... | Creating your account... |
| Please wait | Saving your changes... |
| Processing | Uploading file (45%)... |

## Tone Guidelines

### Professional (SaaS, B2B)
- Clear and direct
- Focus on business outcomes
- Avoid jargon unless targeting experts
- Use contractions (you're, don't, it's)

### Friendly (Consumer, Creative)
- Warm and approachable
- Use "you" and "your"
- Light humor is okay
- Emojis in moderation (not as icons)

### Technical (Developer Tools)
- Precise terminology
- Code snippets where relevant
- Assume technical knowledge
- Focus on capabilities

## Localization Notes

### English
- Use sentence case for buttons and labels
- Use title case for product names
- Avoid idioms and cultural references
- Write for 8th-grade reading level

### Chinese
- Use concise characters
- Avoid direct translations of English idioms
- Adapt examples to local context
- Use proper punctuation (，。instead of , .)

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid these copy mistakes:

1. **No all caps**: Use "Get Started" not "GET STARTED"
2. **No periods in buttons**: "Save" not "Save."
3. **No exclamation overload**: One per page maximum
4. **No vague CTAs**: "Click here" tells nothing
5. **No double negatives**: "Don't miss out" is okay, "Don't not subscribe" is not
6. **No internal jargon**: Users don't know your acronyms
7. **No lorem ipsum**: Use realistic placeholder content
